01.

Statement of Purpose

The Civic Chronic exists to meticulously catalog systemic breakdowns in public infrastructure and governance. We present these findings not as mere complaints, but as an irrefutable archive of neglect. Our mission is to transform the dull hum of bureaucratic incompetence into a resonant frequency of accountability.

By documenting the mundane failures—the pothole that celebrated its third birthday, the bridge to nowhere that cost somewhere around $12M—we construct a mirror for civic leadership that reflects an uncomfortable reality.

02.

Data Acquisition Protocols

Our methodology relies on a rigorous ingestion of public records, stalled FOIA requests, and the translation of bureaucratic obfuscation into plain English. We do not invent failures; we merely curate them.

Public Record Scaping

Automated retrieval of city council meeting minutes and budget variance reports.

Redaction Analysis

Determining the severity of a scandal by the quantity of black ink used to hide it.

Every data point is cross-referenced against actual resident experiences. If the data says the road is paved, but a sedan has been swallowed whole by a crater on Main St, we prioritize the sedan.

03.

The 'Truth' Metric

We utilize a proprietary satire index to determine the severity of a civic failure, weighting incompetence against public expenditure. The formula is approximately:

(Cost of Project × Days Delayed) / (Number of Excuses Given)² = Severity Score

Scores exceeding 1,000 are automatically flagged for a "Feature Story." Scores exceeding 10,000 are forwarded to the Department of Irony for immediate processing.
